■ Signal origin

What this is

Terribledevice is one person's obsession with cyberpunk in pop culture — the films, books, anime, music, and everything adjacent that shaped what it meant to be afraid of the future in a specific, neon-lit way.

The name comes from a William Gibson short story. It felt right. The site is run by one person with too many opinions about Neuromancer, Akira, and Tactical Neural Implant, and not enough hours in the day to write about all of them.

Reviews are short-form and opinionated. Scores exist but they are not the point. The writing is.

■ Scoring system

How scores work

Signal Production & craft

Does the execution serve the work? Sound design, prose, direction — whatever the medium demands, done with intent.

Architecture Composition & structure

How is it built? Does it move with purpose — escalating, breathing, collapsing where it should?

Current Energy & drive

Does it move you — physically, psychically? Momentum, tension, the thing that makes you stay.

Atmosphere World & mood

Does it build a place? The best work in this space makes you feel like you're somewhere specific.

Signal-to-Noise Originality & distinction

Does it have a voice? A reason to exist beyond competent genre execution?
