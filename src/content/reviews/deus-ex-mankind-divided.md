Deus Ex: Mankind Divided was released in August 2016 on all major gaming platforms, including PlayStation, Xbox, and PC. This game serves as the sequel to the critically acclaimed Human Revolution, continuing the story of the titular character, Adam Jensen, who is once again thrust into a world of intricate conspiracies and powerful augmentations.

In my opinion, it's a great follow-up to Human Revolution because it successfully builds upon the foundation laid by the previous game. The storyline is compelling, with intricate plots and well-developed characters that keep players engaged. The graphics and gameplay mechanics have also seen significant improvements, offering a more immersive experience. Overall, Mankind Divided expands on the themes of its predecessor by exploring complex moral dilemmas and providing players with even more freedom of choice in how they approach various missions and challenges.

**Prague is the game's genuine achievement.** Deus Ex: Mankind Divided's hub city is the most densely realized urban environment Eidos has built — layered, textured, and politically legible in a way that turns the setting itself into an argument. The augmented segregation the game depicts is not subtle, but it is coherent, and walking through Prague's divided districts makes the theme visceral in a way that no cutscene could.

The mechanical improvements are real and meaningful. Breach and the new augmentation system give Jensen more options without inflating the design. The side missions are more varied than Human Revolution's, and several of them are among the best writing in either game.

> The world has changed, Jensen. What about you?

Where Mankind Divided stumbles is narrative scope. The story feels like setup for a third installment that has not materialized, and the ending lands without resolution. Human Revolution told a complete story. This one does not, and that absence is felt.

That said, for the 25+ hours it offers before the abrupt close, Mankind Divided is a serious, thoughtful entry in the series and a legitimate continuation of what Human Revolution started.
