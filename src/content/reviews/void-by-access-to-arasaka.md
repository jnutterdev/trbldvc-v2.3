When I think of cyberpunk soundtracks, my mind doesn't immediately jump to the wailing and distorted guitars often associated with bands like Nine Inch Nails. Instead, I envision the intricately crafted and precisely cut-up electronic sounds produced by artists such as Access to Arasaka. These artists create atmospheric and immersive soundscapes that truly capture the essence of the cyberpunk genre. Interestingly, the name Access to Arasaka is derived from the well-known RPG Cyberpunk, which further emphasizes the deep connection between the auditory experience and the thematic elements of this unique and captivating genre.

**void(); is the record that defines what electronic cyberpunk sounds like.** Not cinematic noir, not industrial aggression — something colder and more procedural, like signal processing as emotional language. The glitch textures don't decorate the music; they are the music, each cut and artifact a structural choice rather than an accident.

The project has a clear aesthetic philosophy: that the city's soundscape is built from fragmented data, transmission noise, and the spaces between signals. Access to Arasaka works in those spaces. The record is dense with micro-events that reward close listening — layer on layer of texture that resolves into something ambient on the surface and intricate underneath.

> The sound of a network dreaming.

For anyone building a sense of what cyberpunk sounds like as a genre, this is the record that answers the question without falling back on genre clichés. It doesn't sound like any particular film's soundtrack. It sounds like the underlying condition.
