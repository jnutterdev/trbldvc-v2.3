Karl Meisel has been building Manufactura records since the mid-nineties with a consistency that most artists in the power noise space would find exhausting. Substanz T:6 is not his most technically ambitious record. It is, however, his most controlled — and in this genre, control is the whole game.

**Power noise is not a subtle form.** The instruments are feedback loops, overdriven oscillators, and rhythmic distortion at volumes that register as physical events. What separates competent practitioners from essential ones is what they do with the space between the assaults — whether the dynamics serve anything, whether the architecture implies a purpose.

> This is not music that wants you comfortable. It wants you awake.

Substanz T:6 opens with a title track that does exactly what the name suggests: substancial, tonnage-level noise structured into something that almost resembles a groove. The kick pattern is buried under so much processing that you feel it more than hear it. By the third repetition you are locked in, and that is where the record keeps you for forty-three minutes.

The production is cleaner than you might expect. Meisel understands that harshness is more devastating when the signal path is precise — that a well-recorded wall of noise lands harder than a muddy one. **Every frequency on this record is where it is on purpose.** Nothing bleeds into nothing accidentally. That kind of control at these signal levels is genuinely rare.

Where the album earns its score is in its refusal to apologize or gesticulate. There are no moments of relief designed to make the brutality more palatable. The record commits completely, and that commitment — the patience of a machine that doesn't know how to stop — is what elevates it beyond competent genre execution.

If Substanz T:6 has a limitation, it's that its unity of purpose becomes its ceiling. There is not a great deal of variation in emotional range across the runtime. For listeners who need that variation, the record will read as monotonous. For those who don't, it is very close to definitive.
