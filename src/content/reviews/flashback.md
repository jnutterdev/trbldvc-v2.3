Flashback is a critically acclaimed action platformer video game, originally released in 1992 by the French game development company Delphine Software. Widely regarded as one of the best video games of that era, it captivated players with its engaging gameplay and innovative design.

**The graphics were particularly noteworthy**, as they utilized a unique rotoscope animation technique that brought fluid and realistic movement to the characters, setting a new standard for visual storytelling in video games. Conrad Hart moves with a weight and fluidity that most contemporaries couldn't match — the animation alone communicated genre and tone before a word of dialogue was spoken.

The soundtrack, though minimalistic, was masterfully composed and added an atmospheric depth to the experience. The sci-fi setting — Earth colonies, alien conspiracy, an amnesiac protagonist piecing together a buried identity — gave the game narrative ambition unusual for a platformer of its era. Flashback knew what kind of story it was telling.

> You remember everything except what matters.

The level design is deliberate and methodical. Conrad's movement set is limited by design, and the puzzles and platforming are built around those limits rather than despite them. This constraint gives the game a coherence that more freeform action games lack — when you solve something, it's because you understood the space.

In 2018, to celebrate its 25th anniversary, a remastered version of the game was released by the original studios on the Nintendo Switch. The enhanced graphics and gameplay improvements were designed to offer an enriched experience. But the original holds its own on its own terms — the rotoscoped animation is a period achievement that no upscaling fully replaces.
