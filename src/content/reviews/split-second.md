Kicking off the first post with a '90s classic. Released in 1992, Split Second is not quite the typical cyberpunk film, but it does have a lot of the aesthetics and attitude you typically find in the genre, with a healthy mix of sci-fi and horror. Rutger Hauer (Blade Runner, Blind Fury) stars as a cop surviving on "anxiety, coffee, and chocolate", who's hunting down a demonic serial killer creature that killed his partner years ago. Kim Cattrall (Big Trouble in Little China) also stars alongside as the wife of his ex-partner. The setting of the film takes place in a futuristic, dystopian London, where the streets are flooded with water due to the effects of global warming.

**The film wears its influences openly.** You can feel Blade Runner in the rain-soaked streets, Alien in the creature design, and buddy cop films in the relationship between Hauer and his partner. None of this is pretending to be original — it's a genre pastiche made by people who clearly loved those films, working with a fraction of the budget.

What carries it is Rutger Hauer. He plays Detective Harley Stone as a man running on caffeine and barely contained rage, and he commits to it completely. The role requires someone who can make "I want to eat its heart" feel like a reasonable thing to say, and Hauer delivers. The chemistry with his partner adds genuine comedy to what would otherwise be a straight genre exercise.

> Bigger guns. We need bigger guns.

The flooded London setting does a lot of atmospheric work on a limited budget. You don't need to explain the world when the streets are underwater and the architecture is decaying around your characters — the dystopia is already there in the production design. The film understands that genre atmosphere comes cheap if you shoot the right locations.

I'm not a big fan of horror, but what I like most about the film is that it has the foundation of a buddy cop movie, with elements of sci-fi and some comedy. Even though the movie is somewhat low-budget, it carries natural charm through Hauer and offers cool visuals and themes. Overall, it's a great movie if you can find it.
